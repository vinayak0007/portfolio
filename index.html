Health Literacy


Health literacy is the ability to read, comprehend, understand, and use healthcare information to make appropriate health decisions and follow instructions for treatment. Patients (or their caregivers) who have high health literacy have confidence and awareness regarding their medication resulting in a low chance to misinterpret medication guidelines. In general, those people acquire information about medication by reading the medicine label. However, such existing designs still refer the patient to the direction for use for complete information. Even when it is easy for people to find and access directions for use (e.g., on the Internet), they might have challenges reading, comprehending and processing detailed directions for use because the information can be several pages long.
Supervisor: Prof. Nikola banovic, University of Michigan






Information Design in Medicine Labels


A lot of information has to be presented in a small space in case of medicine labels. This research work carries out flaws in the design of information in medicine labels. The Gaze behavior of participants is recorded and is analyzed. Further, factors affecting information acquisition by participants from the medicine label is discussed and possible solutions are proposed.

I have presented this work at the India HCI 2018 Conference in the Student Research Consortium category.

Splitter | Android Game


SPLITTER, an android game is one of my projects with the Mobile Development Group, IITR. The idea was to create a 2D game that is easy to use but hard to master. These days 2D games are getting out of trend with the introduction of 3D games like PUBG but some 2D games like Angry Birds still find a way to attract game lovers.
Our project started with the characterization of a space-ship that could split in case an obstacle comes in front. The difficulty level of the game increases with an increase in the score of the player. 2D games still remain unique in the gaming industry because of the simplicity they provide. Thus, I had to bring out an easy-to-use interface for the game. A minimal gesture user interface which could be operated quickly was to be designed. At present, the game has a 4.7 rating with over 5k downloads. You can check it out on Google Play.

Portable fabrication Mechanisms


The goal is to make models fabricate on a different machine independent of material and material thickness. This is really exciting that we can take a model from the internet or make it by ourselves and fabricate in on any material or any material thickness. To naively cut a model made for a fabrication machine that is slightly different from your own, likely fails. In particular, in the context of subtractive fabrication, small variations in kerf (cut width of a laser cutter), material stiffness or material thickness result in models that break or fall apart. The software we are developing instead makes models robust to such variations, and thus guarantees the models to work even when changing to new fabrication machines or using files from the web.

Virtual Way-Finding


The project was basically focused on analyzing human attention patterns while navigating through a virtual 3D map of an area. A 3D map of Salt Lake City, Kolkata was developed using Google Sketch Up. Participants were given eye-trackers with was further calibrated with their eye. The whole setup was to compare navigation in real world and the virtual environment. Human perceptions and experience often are deciding while finding ways. Several factors affect decision making such as source, destination, previous experience and our own preferences. Our main focus was to find the extent to which spatial positioning influence way-finding. During the experimentation, it was found that sometimes a path might be chosen to avoid discomfort (like to avoid traffic, noise etc.). Participants were invited from different age groups, gender, financial groups to get better understanding of decision making while way-finding. After navigating through the virtual 3D map, participants were asked to draw a rough map of the area. They were asked various questions to check what information they have in their memory either consciously or subconsciously and their response time was also noted for further analysis.

The Joy of Giving


Working as Intern in UI/UX division of PSTakeCare was a whole new experience for me in the first year summers. PSTakeCare are a young team of passionate graduates & college students from IIT Bombay and ICT backed by medical professionals and healthcare experts.

My task was to renovate the PSTakeCare website. A lot of hospitals were connected with the startup to cater better health facilities to patients but the operations were not shown on the website. Some of the surgeries offered by the startup were Bone & Joint surgeries, Obstetrics & Gynaecology, Heart Disease, Eye surgery and Cancer Treatment. Therefore, my focus was to design an interface which would clearly state the operations and surgeries offered by the firm. We had to keep experience of user light while going through website despite of it being filled with surgery names. A lot of research was done to find a suitable theme for the website and think of possible sections in it followed by wire-framing and prototyping.
